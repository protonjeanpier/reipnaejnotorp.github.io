I recently told a good friend of mine that I was trying not to use ChatGPT and instead have been using the French Mistral AI. I explained that this is due to the way the US has been antagonizing its allies, specifically by threatening to annex Canada & Greenland and toying with the idea of leaving NATO. Thus, I am trying to decrease my dependence on corporations that pay taxes back to the US government and will instead consider my European options more, especially in the digital space. It seemed that he had not been paying attention to how our country's international relations have devolved over the past few years. Anyways, he was surprised about me not wanting to use ChatGPT and commented that I was being "anti-US"[^1].

[^1]: OpenAI is a private corporation anyway, and does not (and should not) have direct ties to the US government in the first place.

> Anti-US, you say? What does it mean to be anti-US? Or pro-US? Or... what does the US even stand for nowadays?

---
There comes a time where a young man (or woman) has to decide who he wants to be and what he stands for. What does he value? What does he not value? How will he stand for his values? These are all important questions that will guide him for the rest of his life. It will define how he interacts with the world and what legacy he leaves behind.

---
I, for one, am not loyal to any one particular country. I am loyal to my values. When a country's government and its actions are standing directly against many of my values, I have no reason to support that country anymore. If I did, **that would be a violation of my integrity**. What good are my values if I don't stand up for them? Without my values and integrity, I am nothing. I am proud that I'm doing my best to stand for my values. **I know what I stand for**.

If the US changes course and starts reflecting my values again, of course I might reconsider what products and services I use. But rest assured: there is no way in hell that I'm letting some pledge of allegiance or oath of allegiance tell me who I am and what I stand for. I can define that for myself, and I encourage my fellow Americans to do the same and think about what they actually stand for.
